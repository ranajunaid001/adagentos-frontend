Understanding Your Metrics

Key Metrics Explained

ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)

  • How much revenue you get for every dollar spent
  • Example: 5x ROAS = $5 revenue for every $1 spent
  • Higher is better

CPM (Cost Per Thousand Impressions)

  • How much it costs to reach 1,000 people
  • Example: $0.50 CPM = 50 cents per 1,000 views
  • Lower is better for awareness

CPA (Cost Per Acquisition)

  • How much it costs to get one conversion
  • Example: $2.50 CPA = costs $2.50 to get one customer
  • Lower is better

CTR (Click-Through Rate)

  • Percentage of people who click your ad after seeing it
  • Example: 2% CTR = 2 out of 100 people clicked
  • Higher is better

Video Completion Rate

  • Percentage of people who watch your entire video
  • Shows engagement levels at different points (3s, 25%, 50%, 100%)
  • Higher is better

How Budget Recommendations Work [IMPROVED]

When Adding Budget

We use a performance-based allocation strategy:

  • 50% goes to your top performer
  • 30% goes to your second-best performer
  • 20% is distributed among other segments

When Cutting Budget

We now provide specific platform recommendations:

  • Identifies exact platforms and amounts to cut
  • Example: "Cut $20k from Facebook (2x ROAS), $15k from Snapchat (2x ROAS)"
  • Always cuts from lowest ROAS platforms first
  • Shows current spend for each platform

When Optimizing

We find opportunities to improve:

  • Move 30-40% from worst to best performers
  • Keep total budget the same
  • Maximize overall performance based on your goal

Technical Details: Agent Prompts

For developers and advanced users, here's how our agents are configured:

Query Generator Prompt Structure

STEP 1: Identify business goal
- AWARENESS: impressions, reach, visibility, CPM, brand
- ENGAGEMENT: clicks, CTR, traffic, visitors, interactions
- CONVERSION: sales, revenue, ROAS, ROI, profit, CPA

STEP 2: Identify query type
- DEEP_DIVE: Single platform analysis
- COMPARISON: Multi-entity with GROUP BY
- OVERVIEW: Aggregated totals
- EXECUTIVE_SUMMARY: Strategic single entity view

SPECIAL HANDLING FOR BUDGET QUESTIONS:
- Always use GROUP BY platform
- Include: platform, roas, spend, revenue
- Order by ROAS ASC for cuts (worst first)

FOLLOW-UP DETECTION:
- Pronouns: "there", "it", "that", "those"
- References: "the best one", "the top performer"
- Maintains context from previous questions

Answer Generator Prompt Structure

Goal-based metric prioritization:
- AWARENESS: Lead with impressions and CPM
- ENGAGEMENT: Lead with CTR and clicks
- CONVERSION: Lead with ROAS and revenue

Formatting rules:
- Bold numbers: **$75,000**, **5x**, **2.5%**
- Bold platforms: **TikTok**, **Instagram**
- Use ‚Üí arrows for lists
- No markdown tables

Strategy instructions:
- Budget ADD: Allocate by performance tiers
- Budget CUT: Specific platforms with amounts
  Example: "Cut $20k from Snapchat (4x ROAS)"
- REALLOCATE: Move 30-40% to optimize

Executive Summary Structure:
- Performance Status: [Strong/Moderate/Needs Attention]
